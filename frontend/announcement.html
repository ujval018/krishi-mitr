Laws & Regulations Regarding MSP

MSP (Minimum Support Price) is the minimum rate at which the government purchases crops from farmers. It is decided by the CACP (Commission for Agricultural Costs and Prices) under the Ministry of Agriculture.

As of now, MSP is not legally binding. Farmers have been demanding a legal guarantee for MSP to ensure that no trade takes place below the government-declared rate.

Discussions are ongoing in Parliament about a proposed law that would mandate MSP as the minimum legal price for all crop transactions, helping protect farmers from exploitation.
